title: "Wallet dashboard"
description: "View balances, send and receive SOL, and swap SOL for USDC from a single screen."
---

The wallet dashboard is the main stage for your wallet after you sign in. From a single screen you can monitor live balances, move SOL between addresses, share your public address to receive funds, and swap SOL for USDC on Solana devnet.

## Prerequisites

- A wallet keypair generated through the sign-in flow. The dashboard redirects to the home page if no keypair is loaded.
- Devnet SOL in the wallet. Fund a new address from the [Solana devnet faucet](https://faucet.solana.com/) before sending or trading.

## Live balances

The dashboard shows two balances side by side:

- **SOL balance** — the native Solana balance for your public address, fetched from the configured RPC endpoint.
- **USDC balance** — the balance of the devnet USDC token account associated with your address. New wallets show `0.00` until a swap or transfer creates the token account.

Both balances refresh automatically every 15 seconds while the dashboard is open, and they re-fetch immediately after a successful send or trade so the values you see stay current without a manual reload.

## Supported actions

The dashboard exposes four modes from the action panel: **view**, **send**, **receive**, and **trade**. Switching modes keeps your keypair loaded — only the form on screen changes.

### View

The default mode. Use it to monitor balances at a glance. No input is required.

### Send SOL

Use send mode to transfer SOL to another Solana address.

1. Switch the dashboard to **Send**.
2. Paste the recipient's Solana public address into the **Recipient** field.
3. Enter the **Amount** of SOL to send. The amount must be greater than `0` and less than or equal to your current SOL balance.
4. Confirm the transaction. The dashboard signs the transfer with your local keypair, submits it through the configured RPC endpoint, and shows the resulting transaction signature on success.

After the transfer confirms, the form resets, the dashboard returns to view mode, and both balances refresh.

### Receive SOL

Receive mode displays your full Solana public address along with a copy button.

1. Switch the dashboard to **Receive**.
2. Copy the address with the copy control, or share the shortened preview with the sender.
3. Wait for the sender's transaction to confirm — the SOL balance updates on the next 15-second refresh.

You do not need to take any other action to receive SOL. Any address holding a keypair on the same Solana cluster can receive transfers.

### Trade SOL for USDC

Trade mode swaps SOL for devnet USDC using the Orca Whirlpools SOL/USDC pool for pricing.

1. Switch the dashboard to **Trade**.
2. Enter the amount of SOL you want to swap. The amount must be greater than `0` and less than or equal to your current SOL balance.
3. Wait for the live quote. The dashboard fetches a quote from the Orca Whirlpools pool roughly 500 ms after you stop typing and displays the estimated USDC output before you commit.
4. Confirm the trade. The dashboard signs and submits the swap with your local keypair and shows the resulting transaction signature on success.

After the swap confirms, the form resets, the dashboard returns to view mode, and both SOL and USDC balances refresh.

## Devnet limitations

The wallet runs against Solana devnet by default, which constrains the trade flow:

- **Single pool.** Quotes and swaps use one hard-coded Orca Whirlpools SOL/USDC pool on devnet. There is no route discovery across multiple pools or aggregators, so liquidity and pricing reflect that single pool only.
- **Devnet USDC mint.** The dashboard reads and receives USDC at the devnet USDC mint. Devnet USDC has no relationship to mainnet USDC and has no real-world value.
- **Devnet RPC.** Transactions are submitted through the devnet RPC endpoint. Confirmation times, fees, and reliability differ from mainnet.
- **Trade direction.** Only SOL → USDC swaps are supported from the dashboard; the reverse direction is not exposed.

Use the dashboard on devnet to explore the flows end to end. Do not treat devnet balances or swap rates as representative of mainnet conditions.
