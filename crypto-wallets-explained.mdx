## What is a crypto wallet?

A crypto wallet is software (or hardware) that stores the cryptographic keys used to access and control assets on a blockchain. Despite the name, wallets don't actually hold coins — the coins live on-chain. The wallet holds the **keys** that prove ownership and let you sign transactions.

## Keys and addresses

Every wallet is built on a **keypair**:

- **Public key / address** — a shareable identifier other people use to send you assets. On Solana, this looks like `9xQeWv...A1b2`.
- **Private key** — a secret value that authorizes spending. Anyone with the private key controls the funds.
- **Seed phrase** — a human-readable backup (typically 12 or 24 words) that can regenerate the private key. Lose it and you lose access; share it and you lose your funds.

<Warning>
  Never share your private key or seed phrase. No legitimate service, support agent, or airdrop will ever ask for it.
</Warning>

## Custodial vs. non-custodial

|  | Custodial | Non-custodial |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Who holds the keys** | A third party (e.g., an exchange) | You |
| **Recovery** | Password reset via the provider | Seed phrase only |
| **Trust model** | Trust the custodian | Trust yourself |
| **Examples** | Coinbase, Binance accounts | Phantom, Solflare, this app |

Non-custodial wallets give you full control, which also means full responsibility for backups.

## Hot vs. cold wallets

- **Hot wallets** are connected to the internet (browser extensions, mobile apps, web wallets). Convenient for daily use and dApp interactions.
- **Cold wallets** are offline (hardware devices like Ledger, or paper backups). Best for long-term storage of larger balances.

A common pattern: keep a small spending balance in a hot wallet and the rest in cold storage.

## How a transaction works

1. You build a transaction (e.g., "send 1 SOL to address X").
2. Your wallet **signs** it locally with your private key.
3. The signed transaction is broadcast to the network.
4. Validators verify the signature and include it in a block.
5. The on-chain balances update — irreversibly.

Because transactions are final, double-check the recipient address and amount before signing.

## Networks and tokens

A single wallet address on Solana can hold:

- **SOL** — the native token used for fees and staking.
- **SPL tokens** — fungible tokens like USDC, BONK, or any project token.
- **NFTs** — non-fungible tokens, also implemented as SPL tokens.

Most chains also have **testnets** (like Solana devnet) where tokens have no real value, which is perfect for learning and development.

## Staying safe

- Back up your seed phrase offline, in more than one secure location.
- Verify URLs before connecting your wallet — phishing sites mimic real dApps.
- Review every transaction prompt; malicious sites can request approvals that drain your wallet.
- Use a separate "burner" wallet for experimenting with new dApps.
